Simple Robotics - Incomplete

Created : 2020-12-11 | Author : Fish
Let's start by a simple definition of a robot, a machine to do human task to lessen manpower or make more efficient. The simplest form of them are to replace menial works (moving stuff, monitoring, etc) currently we have all the resource available to make robot to a lot of things, even complicated one but their development seems to be slow, why is that?
  • Arduino makes creating robots way easier than before
  • Sensors are very cheap and there's a lot of their variants (water level, distance, heat, camera, etc)
  • Pre-assembled electrical modules are avalable, allowing creation with basic knowledge of electricity (Voltage booster, Motor controller, etc)
  • 3D printer are getting cheaper and better, but it requires some skill with 3D modelling to build your own model but it's quite easy actually
Maybe the overcomplication of development is one to blame, see my homepage about that, stuffs gets more complicated making it harder to a lot of people to understand and do, and people tend to act when the danger is already in front of their face, WW1, WW2, and Cold War accelerates a lot of research, having a competition forces us to race the enemy first before they reach us by getting an advantage at a technology, but there's no competition today, humanity is since is on full procastination mode just taking it easy because there is no threat to not do it, as quoted from Johnny Got His Gun.

"War has a various meanings to various persons, to the scientist, war means that he is actually set free to accomplish his most brilliant and most imaginative enterprises. For example, in previous wars, each injury has resulted in a very serious loss to the taxpayers the loss of a most expensively trained soldier or a fighting unit as we call him now. However, in the next war we shall be able to repair and deliver that same fighting unit to the frontline trenches in three weeks or even less. And all bacause of the radical new techniques which this young man has taught us."

  • Haber-Bosch process creates fertilizers and explosives, allowing the German Empire to produce munitions while fertilizer greatly improved agriculture
  • The tank, in order for it to do well we need a better engine, better armor, better guns and everything. There's a lot of work and research just to make the perfect war machine, but of course the research will benefit others when the war is over.
  • Aeronautics accelerates very fast, when first used both sides began so see it's benefits so they race each other to make the best airplane.
  • And the most important, Nuclear physics, when the American first used it the whole world was shaken by its power, not for long the Soviet Unions makes their own, and then it got bigger yields and more efficient, this progress directly increased our understanding in particle physics.
Got sidetracked for a bit back to the main topic, those points SHOULD make someone that is interested in robotics do their jobs easier, for now let's list some avtivites that can be automated by robots and how one can learn to create them.

Farming

We know by now not a lot of people are interested in it while the world population keeps on increasing and farmers are decreasing, by that farming is the most crucial sector to automate bacause no one is interested in it, but then unforseen consequences might follow if we do this path, some robots have been created for this purpose
  • Asirobots autonomous tractor - Same concept as driverless truck, the technical might require some knowledge to operate.
  • FarmBot - The price and scale might let you down but this system has a potential.
  • Viscon Hydroponics - Proven and they already have a running farm, might be better for specialized crop since hydroponic have some advantage to soil.
  • You might be able to find more in the internet some might be very small scale but there's always a lesson to learn from them.
